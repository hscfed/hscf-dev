About HSCF

HSCF is a homeschool family support group that has been serving the Christian home school community in Edmonton since the 1980s.

Visit our Activities page to learn more about HSCF events and if you would like to become a member please visit the Join HSCF page.

Activities Join HSCF

Member families come from a wide variety of church affiliations, but are united in our statement of faith and goals & philosophy that are based on Biblical principles.

Mission Statement

HSCF seeks to facilitate building a Christian community for homeshooling families by providing opportunities for their involvement in meetings and activities and supporting parents in their responsibility of directing their children's education.

Statement of Faith

  1. I believe the Bible is the inspired word of God and is my supreme and final authority.
  2. I believe there is one God eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son and Holy Spirit.
  3. I believe our Lord Jesus is the Son of God, conceived of the Holy Spirit to Mary, a virgin. He died on a cross. The shedding of His blood was the atonement for my sins. I believe He was raised from the dead and ascended to heaven - where He is my advocate at the Father's right hand.
  4. I believe in the personal and literal return of Jesus Christ for His Church.
  5. I believe Jesus Christ is the only way to heaven and that a person must receive Him as their Saviour and Lord.
  6. I believe in life after death - either in heaven or in hell.

Goals & Objectives

  1. The goal of the Association is to serve as the unifying body for Christian Homeschools and share its personnel and resources to the best possible advantage of all.
  2. The objective of the Association shall be to encourage and edify this God-ordained system of education that is distinctly Christian.

Philosophy

We believe that:

  1. God is the source of all truth and this must be the unifying principle in education.
  2. Christian parents should have the option of quality home-centered education for their children which is distinctly Christian.
  3. The ultimate aim of Christian home education is to develop the abilities of the individual in order that he or she might fulfill God's will and make a positive contribution to the Church and society.
