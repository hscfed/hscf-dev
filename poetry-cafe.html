Poetry Cafe

We hope you will join us for an evening of creativity, inspiration, beautiful words... poetry!

Below, you will find information on how to prepare for a poetry presentation and how to do your very best.

Come and share a passage of literature that has moved you, thrilled you, captured your imagination, or transported you to another world...

HSCF Members, come take the stage at the Poetry Cafe!!

First Things First:

  • Choose a poem that you enjoy!
  • Memorize the poem - know it really well so you are comfortable saying it in front of other people.
  • Practice in front of a mirror so you see what your audience will see - practice good eye contact!
  • Please keep recitations close to 2 minutes. If you have chosen a very long poem, choose your favourite section of the poem that will fit within the time limit.

Practice! Practice! Practice!

  • Start with the title of the poem and the poet's name, for example: "Daffodils by William Wordsworth".
  • Look at everyone.
  • Speak loudly and clearly so everyone can hear.
  • At the end of your poem, pause to let the words settle over the audience, then walk away.
  • Let the words of the poem do the emotional work - be the narrator; tell us the story.

One Final Thing:

You are welcome to bring along a prompter (a person seated in the front of the audience who supplies a forgotten word or line to the performer during their recitations) in case you need a little help. We will have a designated seat for just that individual. Be sure to provide them with a copy of your poem.
