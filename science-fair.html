Science & Heritage Fair

HSCF hosts a Science & Heritage Fair every year! Students may choose to do a Science Fair Project or a Heritage Fair Project.

SCIENCE FAIR PROJECTS are fabulous presentations that showcase a science topic of the student's choice. Science projects are presented in one of two ways:

  • Experiment Format: (eg. Which Leftovers in my Fridge Grow Blue Mold?) The experiment format should use the scientific method including a clear purpose, hypothesis, procedure, materials list, data or observations, results, analysis and conclusions.
  • Model Format: (eg. the Lifecycle of a Frog / Simple Machines / Rocks) The model format may also be known as a "study"; it does not require the scientific method.

HERITAGE FAIR PROJECTS are fabulous presentations that showcase a Canadian social studies topic. They should have a Canadian heritage theme (historical or geographical). The topic could be specific to one period of time, or show development over time. These projects should also demonstrate an understanding of research skills (e.g. using primary & secondary sources).

Students may work individually or with others on the same project. The display dimensions should be no more than 2m wide x 1m deep. The display must be titled and credit must be given to resources used. (Often younger students will bring along the books they used or write a list of titles, while older students may use this opportunity to include a more detailed bibliography).

Students will receive verbal and written feedback on their displays from our wonderful team of judges. To make this event superb, each family is asked to provide at least one judge. This could be you, another parent, grandparent, friend, aunt, uncle, pastor, etc. that has a genuine interest in listening to the children.

Each child will have one judge come to talk to them, as well as have time to talk with other parents and kids about their project. Judges will be looking for ways the students have integrated Biblical truths or references into their project.
